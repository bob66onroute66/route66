S anta Fe

We started our day cruising around Santa Fe, checking out the sites and multiple gift shops. This is a place you could easily spend an entire day or more, just taking in the attractions and history.

The architecture is unique (very New Mexico). The streets seemed narrow, and finding a place to park wasn’t that easy. We ended up parking in a parking lot behind a building and walked along the streets looking in the various shops. Some shops were selling a lot of artistic jewelry. We saw a lot of blue turquoise items. It kind of reminded me of the Native American jewelry sold around the Wisconsin Dells area, when I was a kid. Some if it, pretty pricey. A fun place to look around.

From here, we headed to TinkerTown!

TinkerTown

TinkerTown; what can I say about TinkerTown? I think this is one of those places you either love or hate it. This is a place full of nostalgia, with tons of beautiful, ancient, miniature figures, items,

displays, etc. The place is huge and the displays are mind boggling. This is truly a hoarders paradise. I could spend an entire day there, but like I said, some people (like my wife, Dorreen) could find it kind of creepy. Kind of like creepy, evil, Chucky dolls: but I loved it and think no matter what you think it’s worth a stop.

6 6 Diner In Albuquerque

This is a very cool diner. If ya wanna go back to the late 50’s, 60’s, this IS IT! (Remember, I’m 66 and when I was a kid a lot of restaurants were like this….there was no McDonalds, yet). The blue uniforms the ladies wear are reminiscent of the times. Oh yeah; the food and service is great.

Note: We actually accidentally stopped here, again, on our way back from the Outlaw Country Cruise West. We were cruising through Albuquerque, looking for a place to eat. We saw this place, not remembering it, stopped in got a table. After sitting down and seeing the girls in blue uniforms, we realized we were here before. (We’re old). The service and food was great, again.

G reat Day

We ended our day at the Roadway Inn in Gallup, New Mexico. It was good and cheap!
